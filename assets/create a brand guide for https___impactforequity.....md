Based on the content, history, and messaging of **Impact for Equity** (formerly BPI), here is a comprehensive brand guide designed to maintain consistency in how the organization presents itself to the public, partners, and stakeholders.

# **📘 Impact for Equity: Brand Guide**

## **1\. Brand Overview & History**

**Who We Are:** Impact for Equity is a public-interest law and policy center combating racial, economic, and social injustice for and with communities most disadvantaged by systemic inequities. We are an unflinching catalyst for transformational change across Chicago and Illinois.  
**Our Legacy:** Founded in 1969 as Businessmen for the Public Interest (BPI), the organization rebranded in 2023 to **Impact for Equity** to better reflect its core values and unyielding commitment to advancing racial, economic, and social justice.

## **2\. The Brand Compass (Mission, Vision, & Tagline)**

* **Tagline:** *Combating Injustice Together.*  
* **Alternative Core Slogans:**  
  * *Equity for All is Why We're Here.*  
  * *We Put Lasting Change First.*  
  * *An Unflinching Catalyst for Equity.*  
* **Our Mission:** To combat systemic barriers to equity and to fight for racial, economic, and social justice.  
* **Our Vision (The End Goal):** A world where every person can live their fullest life in a thriving community free of systemic barriers.

## **3\. Core Values**

Our work and partnerships are governed by an uncompromising commitment to five core values:

* **Collaboration:** Build collective power by working respectfully and sharing power.  
* **Determination:** Honor the struggle through humility, tenacity, innovation, and risk-taking.  
* **Effectiveness:** Strive for results that lead to enduring change.  
* **Equity:** Require justice and fairness in access, process, and results.  
* **Integrity:** Approach our work with honesty, intentionality, and transparency.

## **4\. Key Focus Areas & The "Theory of Change"**

Impact for Equity focuses primarily on the intersection of two overarching sectors, understanding that they work in tandem to create interlocking systems of injustice:

1. **Housing:** Removing barriers and promoting authentic housing choices across the Chicago region.  
2. **Criminal Legal System:** Fighting for equitable outcomes for those impacted by the criminal legal system in Illinois.

**The Strategic Beliefs (Theory of Change):**

* *Any system that dehumanizes any of us dehumanizes us all.*  
* We are guided by the people most impacted.  
* Systems are at the root of racial, social, and economic injustice. By tackling the systems, we drive change.  
* We work for equity, liberation, and safety across interconnected systems.

## **5\. Brand Voice & Tone**

The voice of Impact for Equity is **fierce, authoritative, collaborative, and empowering.**

* **Fierce & Unflinching:** We do not shy away from calling out centuries of systemic racism and economic discrimination. We speak truth to power.  
* **Collaborative & Humble:** We use language that emphasizes *partnership*. We do not "save" communities; we work "for and with" them. *("Partnership drives everything we do. We learn and grow from everyone we work with.")*  
* **Action-Oriented:** The tone should emphasize forward momentum, utilizing strong verbs (e.g., *Litigating, Advocating, Fostering, Combating, Catalyzing*).  
* **Professional yet Accessible:** As a law and policy center generating in-depth legal research and data analysis, the tone must remain credible, evidence-based, and legally sound, while remaining accessible to the general public and community organizers.

## **6\. Target Audience & Stakeholders**

Our messaging is designed to resonate with several distinct groups:

* **Impacted Communities:** People living the daily realities of inequity, particularly Black people and other people of color in historically marginalized and disinvested communities in Illinois.  
* **Community Partners:** Grassroots organizations, organizers, and change-makers who guide and shape our strategies.  
* **System Stakeholders:** Lawmakers, city/state officials, the Chicago Housing Authority, and police/justice departments who need to be educated, lobbied, or challenged.  
* **Supporters:** Pro-bono legal partners, fellowship applicants, donors, and the broader philanthropic community.

## **7\. Key Messaging Pillars**

When writing about the organization's tools and methodologies, always highlight these core strategies:

* **Partnership Model:** *“Together, we’re making justice a reality in Chicago and beyond.”* Never portray the organization as working in a silo. We bring together people with lived, professional, technical, and legal expertise.  
* **The Revolving Door:** Clearly explain *why* we focus on housing and the criminal legal system together. (e.g., *“When someone has unstable housing, they are more likely to end up in the criminal legal system. And when they end up in the criminal legal system, they have a harder time finding stable housing.”*)  
* **Using Every Tool Available:** Highlight our multi-disciplinary approach: Legal research, impact litigation, public awareness campaigns, and policy advocacy.

## **8\. Visual Identity & Presentation (Web & Print Guidelines)**

*(Note: As specific hex codes are managed internally, adhere to these general web-based visual principles)*

* **Logo Usage:** The *Impact for Equity* logo should be displayed prominently and cleanly on all materials. It should not be stretched, distorted, or crowded by other visual elements.  
* **Accessibility First:** Colors and typography must adhere to ADA web accessibility standards (high contrast, readable fonts), mirroring the brand’s commitment to "fairness in access."  
* **Imagery:** Use authentic photography that highlights community partnership, grassroots action, housing access, and civic engagement over stock imagery. Focus on real people making a tangible impact.

*This brand guide serves as the foundation for all internal and external communications, ensuring that Impact for Equity speaks with a unified, powerful, and legally credible voice across all platforms.*

01 — Brand Overview

An unflinching catalyst for equity.

Impact for Equity is a public-interest law and policy center combating racial, economic, and social injustice for and with the communities most disadvantaged by systemic inequities — a catalyst for transformational change across Chicago and Illinois.

Who we are

A law and policy center that does not "save" communities — we work for and with them, guided by the people most impacted by inequity.

Our legacy

Founded in 1969 as Businessmen for the Public Interest (BPI), the organization rebranded in 2023 to Impact for Equity — resisting and persisting for more than 55 years.

02 — The Brand Compass

Mission, vision & tagline.

Our Mission

To combat systemic barriers to equity and to fight for racial, economic, and social justice.

Our Vision

A world where every person can live their fullest life in a thriving community free of systemic barriers.

Tagline & core slogans

Combating Injustice Together.
Equity for All is Why We're Here. We Put Lasting Change First. An Unflinching Catalyst for Equity.

Focus areas & theory of change

We work at the intersection of two sectors that create interlocking systems of injustice.

Housing

Removing barriers and promoting authentic housing choices across the Chicago region.

Criminal Legal System

Fighting for equitable outcomes for those impacted by the criminal legal system in Illinois.

“Any system that dehumanizes any of us dehumanizes us all.”

06 — Logo Usage

Protect the mark.

The stepped-bar emblem and wordmark should always appear prominently and cleanly. Give it room to breathe — never stretch, distort, recolor, or crowd it.

Primary — on light
Emblem / favicon
Never stretch or distort

Do

  • Maintain clear space equal to the height of one bar on all sides.
  • Place on white or Equity Navy for maximum contrast.
  • Use the emblem alone for avatars, favicons, and small spaces.

Don't

  • Stretch, skew, or change the logo's proportions.
  • Recolor the marks outside the approved blue palette.
  • Crowd it with text or place it on low-contrast backgrounds.

07 — Brand Voice & Tone

Fierce. Authoritative. Collaborative.

We speak truth to power — and we do it together. Our voice is credible and evidence-based, yet accessible to the general public and community organizers.

Fierce & unflinching

We don't shy away from naming centuries of systemic racism and economic discrimination.

Collaborative & humble

We work for and with communities — we never claim to "save" them.

Action-oriented

Strong verbs drive momentum: litigating, advocating, fostering, combating, catalyzing.

Professional yet accessible

Legally sound and data-driven, but never out of reach for the people we serve.

Say this

  • "Together, we're making justice a reality in Chicago and beyond."
  • "We are guided by the people most impacted."
  • "By tackling the systems, we drive change."

Avoid this

  • Savior language — "we rescue" or "we fix" communities.
  • Portraying the organization as working in a silo.
  • Dense jargon that shuts out community organizers.

08 — Key Messaging Pillars

How we tell the story.

Partnership model

We bring together people with lived, professional, technical, and legal expertise. Never a silo.

The revolving door

Unstable housing leads to the criminal legal system — and that system makes stable housing harder. We tackle both together.

Every tool available

Legal research, impact litigation, public awareness campaigns, and policy advocacy — a multi-disciplinary approach.

“When someone has unstable housing, they are more likely to end up in the criminal legal system. And when they end up in the criminal legal system, they have a harder time finding stable housing.”

09 — Audience & Imagery

Who we speak to.

Impacted communities

People living the daily realities of inequity — particularly Black people and other people of color in historically disinvested Illinois communities.

Community partners

Grassroots organizations, organizers, and change-makers who guide and shape our strategies.

System stakeholders

Lawmakers, city/state officials, the Chicago Housing Authority, and justice departments to educate, lobby, or challenge.

Supporters

Pro-bono legal partners, fellowship applicants, donors, and the broader philanthropic community.

Imagery guidelines

Use authentic photography highlighting community partnership, grassroots action, housing access, and civic engagement — real people making tangible impact, over generic stock imagery. Maintain ADA-level contrast and readable type throughout: fairness in access is a brand value, not just a guideline.
